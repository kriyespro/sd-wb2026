I actually think you're identifying the key business problem.

**Clients do not hire training institutes.**
Clients hire **trusted agencies**.

So your website should **not** present Winning Blueprints as an institute that also does agency work.

It should present Winning Blueprints as a **professional Digital Growth Company** that happens to have an internal **Industry Academy**.

The academy is a **talent pipeline**, not the main business.

# Developer Instructions (Architecture)

## Core Principle

**Build one platform with three isolated experiences sharing the same backend:**

1. **Public Agency Website** (Primary Brand)

   * Target audience: Business owners
   * Goal: Generate leads and close projects
   * No emphasis on students or training on the homepage.

2. **Academy Portal** (Secondary Brand)

   * Target audience: Students and career switchers
   * Goal: Admissions, learning, internship, placement
   * Accessible from `/academy` or `academy.winningblueprints.in`.

3. **Internal Operations Portal**

   * Target audience: Staff, mentors, project managers, interns
   * Goal: Deliver client projects while managing student training.

---

## Multi-Role User System

Implement Role-Based Access Control (RBAC) with separate permissions:

### Agency Roles

* Super Admin
* Director
* Sales Executive
* Project Manager
* Account Manager
* SEO Specialist
* Google Ads Specialist
* Meta Ads Specialist
* Web Developer
* UI/UX Designer
* Content Writer
* QA Specialist

### Academy Roles

* Academy Admin
* Mentor
* Trainer
* Student
* Intern
* Placement Coordinator

### Client Roles

* Business Owner
* Team Member (optional)

Each role should have an independent dashboard and permissions.

---

## Project Assignment Rules

* Every client project must have:

  * One Project Manager (owner)
  * One Senior Specialist (technical lead)
  * Optional Interns/Students assigned for execution
* Students cannot communicate directly with clients unless explicitly authorized.
* All deliverables require mentor approval before they become visible to the client.

---

## Business Philosophy

> **Clients buy expertise. Students earn experience. Winning Blueprints connects the two through a controlled, mentor-led delivery process.**

This positioning lets you market yourself confidently as a **professional digital growth agency**, while your academy becomes a strategic advantage rather than the centerpiece. It's a model similar to how consulting firms and software companies develop talent internally: clients see experienced professionals, while new talent learns under structured supervision.
